
    But soft! what light through yonder window breaks?
    It is the east, and Juliet is the sun!
    Arise fair sun, and kill the envious moon,
    Who is already sick and pale with grief.
    
    The fair creature that doth make my days
    Doth make mine eyes to weep at her sweet face,
    And yet I love her best of all things here.
    
    For beauty's sake, she is so fair,
    That in her presence, I am made to fear,
    Yet she is gentle, kind and true.
    
    Sweet lady, you are more than fair,
    Your beauty doth surpass the stars above,
    And yet your heart is soft as morning dew.
    
    O gentle maid, what words can I use
    To praise thy worth and beauty so divine?
    Thy name is written in the stars above.
    
    What though I be of low degree,
    Yet still I love thee with a heart sincere,
    And will protect thee with my sword and shield.
